Questions the Standard Answers

Every institution arriving at AI governance asks the same questions. The answers are not opinions â they are in the record.

What is an AI decision record â and why does it matter now? +

An AI decision record (AgDR) is a tamper-evident, cryptographically signed log of every autonomous AI decision â capturing who acted, on whose behalf, why, and what the full reasoning chain was, at the exact moment of inference. It matters now because AI systems are making consequential decisions in finance, healthcare, and law â and the question of accountability can no longer wait for the crash.

How do I verify an AgDR record in Python? +

Recompute the BLAKE3 hash of the atomic kernel payload, verify the Ed25519 signature against the Provenance public key, then check the Merkle root against the chain. Any deviation is mathematically detectable. Full Python verification code is in the Verification & Audit Procedure.

What proves record integrity in AI audits? +

Three layers: (1) BLAKE3 hash of every atomic capture, (2) Ed25519 signature at inference time, (3) forward-secret Merkle chain that makes any insertion, deletion, or modification mathematically visible. The record cannot be quietly corrected after a decision goes wrong. That irreversibility is precisely the point.

How does AgDR implement EU AI Act Article 12 and Article 14? +

Article 12 requires high-risk AI systems to maintain automatic event logs throughout their lifetime. AgDR captures every inference atomically at kernel level. Article 14 requires human oversight mechanisms. The Human Delta Chain and FOI Terminal Node provide named, timed, signed human intervention records. AI compliance becomes structural, not retrospective.

How does the NIST AI Risk Management Framework relate to AgDR? +

The NIST AI RMF 1.0 identifies traceability, transparency, and accountability as core properties of trustworthy AI. AgDR implements all three structurally. For organizations already aligned to NIST, AgDR is the technical implementation layer, not a competing framework.

What is the difference between AgDR and a standard audit log? +

Standard audit logs are written retrospectively and can be silently modified. AgDR captures AI decisions atomically at the inference instant â before any post-processing â and signs each record so tampering is mathematically detectable. AgDR also captures the full reasoning chain, human oversight events, and named fiduciary accountability that standard logs structurally cannot provide.
