Statutory Authentication

Statutory Identity.

The PPP Triplet provides the metadata required to authenticate an electronic record under federal law. Source: accountability.ai PPP Protocol specification.

A record without defined intent and author is mere data. For an AI inference to carry legal weight, it must satisfy the requirements of Authentication and Authority. The PPP Protocol (Provenance, Place, Purpose) is the technical enforcement of these statutory pillars, as defined in the AgDR v1.8 specification at accountability.ai.

Provenance

Authenticates source origin and data lineage at the sub-microsecond level via Atomic Kernel Inference. This satisfies the context required for business judgment defense and evidentiary chain of custody under CEA s.31.1.

Place

Logs the specific compute node, hardware environment, and jurisdictional boundary at decision time. Essential for proving system integrity under CEA s.31.2 and data residency compliance.

Purpose

Captures explicit compliance intent and fiduciary alignment at inference instant. Maps to signature requirements under historical commercial law and modern CBCA s.122 duty of care obligations.

By defining Provenance, Place, and Purpose before an inference begins, AgDR ensures the resulting decision record is not a post-hoc reconstruction. It is a contemporaneous, cryptographically sealed record that meets the highest evidentiary standards in Canadian law. Reference: accountability.ai PPP Protocol and AgDR v1.8 specification.

Statutory Alignment

The PPP Triplet directly supports compliance with:

  • Canada Evidence Act s.31.1: Authentication of electronic records through system integrity proof
  • CBCA s.122: Fiduciary duty of care through documented human oversight via the Human Delta Chain
  • PIPEDA: Accountability principle through cryptographic sealing of consent and purpose context

All statutory mappings are derived from the AgDR open standard, stewarded by the Genesis Glass Foundation. Primary reference: accountability.ai PPP Pillars specification.
