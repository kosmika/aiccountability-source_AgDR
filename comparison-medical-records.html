Comparison to Medical Records

AgDR v0.2 vs Medical Records (Electronic Health Records / EHR)
The gold standard for accountability in human decisions — now extended to autonomous agents.

Dimension Medical Records (EHR/EMR) AgDR v0.2 + Atomic Kernel Inference (AKI) Edge for the Mission
Purpose Create a longitudinal, auditable record of clinical decisions for treatment, continuity, and legal protection Create an instantaneous, mathematically indivisible record of every agent decision for accountability and fiduciary protection AgDR prevents harm in real time
Capture Trigger Human entry or system logging after the decision Automatic at the exact inference instant (“i” point) — no human required AgDR captures the decision at the moment of thought
Atomicity Best-effort (notes can be amended) True kernel-level transaction — the entire record is written or never exists AgDR is mathematically stronger
Tamper-Evidence Audit logs and access controls (still editable in some systems) Cryptographic signature + forward-secret Merkle chain from the exact moment of inference AgDR is tamper-proof from birth
What It Records Observations, orders, rationale notes Full reasoning trace + PPP triplet + binary human delta chain ending in Fiduciary Office Intervener AgDR records why with provable intent
Legal Weight (Canada) Extremely high — presumed reliable under PHIPA and Canada Evidence Act Same foundation + cryptographic proof exceeds typical EHR tests AgDR carries equal or greater weight
Accountability Layer Treating physician or nurse as the responsible party Binary human delta escalation ending in catalogued Fiduciary Office Intervener (FOI) AgDR forces clear escalation to existing fiduciary duty
Standard of Care Defines the legal and ethical duty of care in healthcare Elevates the standard of care for autonomous systems to the same level This is the aspiration we stand at the pinnacle of
Scalability & Cost High cost, siloed per institution Near-zero cost, universal across any model or agent AgDR is global infrastructure

Bottom line
Medical records set the standard of care for human decisions in life-critical domains.
AgDR brings that same standard — and in many technical respects a higher one — to autonomous agents.

This is why we stand at the pinnacle of the standard of care that I wish to one day achieve.
