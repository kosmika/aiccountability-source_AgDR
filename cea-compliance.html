Canada Evidence Act (s.31.1)

Establishing the "Digital Original" for Autonomous Systems.

The Best Evidence Rule

Traditional AI logs are retrospective narratives and often considered hearsay. Under the CEA, for a record to be admissible as "Best Evidence," its integrity must be proven from the moment of creation.

AKI Kernel Role

The 950ns AKI Force Interrupt ensures that the signature is baked into the decision at Genesis, satisfying the CEA requirement for contemporaneous record-keeping.

Forensic Integrity

By utilizing BLAKE3 hashing, we provide a mathematical proof of integrity that is admissible in Canadian courts as a reliable representation of the electronic system.

The "Digital Original" Standard

CEA s.31.1 requires that electronic records be proven reliable. AgDR satisfies this by: (1) sealing the record at the exact inference instant via AKI, (2) chaining records in a forward-secret Merkle tree, and (3) providing a Human Delta Chain for audit. This creates a "digital original" that meets the Best Evidence Rule.
